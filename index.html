Agentic system blueprint

An operating system for Corient.

One contract. Seven workflows. Five shared primitives. Everything else compounds.

01 — Domains

Five agents. One contract.

Five agent domains, already deployed. Each one owns a bounded responsibility. None of them call each other directly — they read from a shared contract and emit typed events.

ai-creative
Creative
Static ads, video ads, social content. Orchestrates Runway, HeyGen, ElevenLabs, Nano Banana Pro, FLUX.2 into one production pipeline.
ai-campaigns
Campaigns
Takes approved creative and launches it. Variant factories, A/B, scheduling, spend guardrails. Closes the loop with the learning pipeline.
ai-sales
Sales
Deal context, proposals, hand-off to admin on close. Deterministic pricing, reviewer pass, fresh context.
ai-reports
Reports
Cross-platform metrics, evidence cards, narrative grounded in the brief's success criteria. Half of the learning loop.
ai-admin
Admin
Client cards, access, internal ops. Turns a signed contract into a working engagement without human toil.
02 — Workflows

Seven builds.

Each workflow is a standalone architecture. Each ships value on its own. Built in order, they compound into the full system.

W-01

The contract.

A typed schema, not a document. Created on deal-won. Auto-updates on every signal after. Lives in the client card. Every other workflow reads from it.

Every client has one card. The brief is a Pydantic schema projected into that card — Notion is the human view, Postgres is the source of truth. The workflow has two triggers: deal.won seeds the card and the golden set from scratch; every signal after that appends to the meeting log and classifies the diff. Nothing else works without this.

W-02

Static ad production.

Brief in, full variant matrix out. 2026 frontier models, brand assets conditioned in pre-generation, two-judge vision QA panel.

2026 frontier models handle multi-element composition, text rendering, and brand asset insertion in a single pass. Generate-then-inpaint is 2025 thinking. We pass the client's logo, palette, font samples, and three hero past-winners as references before generation, not after. Inpainting becomes a fallback, not a stage.

W-03

Social content
repurposing.

One long-form source becomes 30+ platform-native pieces. Voice fidelity enforced by per-client golden sets, not generic rubrics.

Extraction is solved. The hard problem is voice fidelity at platform shift — a LinkedIn paragraph and a TikTok hook for the same client must sound like the same brain. The only reliable answer is a per-client golden set of 20 hand-curated examples, scored by embedding distance plus rubric grading. Judges drift. Golden sets do not.

W-04

Compliance
& claims gate.

A hard wall, not a cap. Every generative asset passes through before delivery. This is how agencies avoid getting sued.

Generative ads at scale without a compliance pass is a lawsuit waiting to happen. Beauty claims, financial claims, supplement claims, health claims, restricted categories, Meta's prohibited content list, ASIC/ACCC for AU clients, FTC endorsement rules, age-gating, region-specific supers. Built once at org level so every client inherits the same policy brain.

Pipeline
1
Asset arrives. Final deliverable from W-02, W-03, W-05 or W-06. Before any Gate 2.
Inngest event
2
Claims extraction. Every explicit and implicit claim pulled from copy, voiceover, and on-screen text. Listed structurally.
Claude Sonnet
3
Substantiation cross-check. Each claim cross-referenced against the client's substantiation library (Mem0). Unsupported claims flagged.
Mem0 · Claude
4
Policy classifier — platform + region. Meta, TikTok, Google, LinkedIn prohibited content lists. ACCC/ASIC for AU, FTC for US, ASA for UK. Category-specific rules (alcohol, gambling, finance, health).
Rule engine
5
Auto-remediation or block. Missing legal super → auto-added via Creatomate template layer. Unsupported claim → hard block, human review.
Creatomate
✓
Any flagged item → human compliance review. Hard wall. Cannot be overridden by caps or budgets. Review goes to the account lead, not a random approver.
Slack · Notion
6
Audit log entry. Every asset carries a compliance receipt — claims list, substantiation sources, policy matches, approver. Legally defensible.
Postgres
W-05

Video ad production.

Two pipelines, chosen by brief. Native generative for 80% of output. Segment-based for talking-head UGC only.

Veo 3.1 and Runway Gen-4.5 now handle 8–12 second coherent shots with consistent characters and native audio. For most ad formats this means generating end-to-end shots and assembling two or three — not stitching avatar + b-roll + VO segments. The segment-based pattern is reserved for talking-head and UGC-style formats where the client's CAC depends on a recognisable spokesperson.

W-07

The learning loop.

One shared data pipeline, two surfaces. Client reports are the human view of the same learning that feeds the next round of creative. The compounding engine.

Reports and the creative feedback loop share the same pipeline — platform metrics, element decomposition, win/loss tagging. Splitting them into separate workflows doubles the plumbing and hides the connection. Here they are one. What the agency learns becomes what the agency ships next week. What the client sees is the same truth in narrative form.

Shared pipeline
1
Daily metrics pull. GA4, Meta, TikTok, Google Ads, LinkedIn → canonical MetricPoint → Postgres. Creative-level performance tied to asset_id.
n8n · Postgres
2
Creative element decomposition. Each ad broken into: hook, visual style, CTA, format, hero subject, music bed, opening 1.5 seconds. Structured tags via vision subagent.
Claude vision
3
Win/loss tagging + evidence cards. Win/loss scored against the brief's CPA threshold. Anomalies detected statistically (z-score, 3-week moving average). Each anomaly becomes an evidence card — timestamp, metric, magnitude, tracked context.
Code
Surface A — Priors for the next round of creative
A1
Pattern store update. Two writes: per-client winning patterns (Mem0 namespace) and org-level pattern memory (pgvector). Every winning ad across all clients contributes anonymously.
Mem0 · pgvector
A2
Injected into next W-02/W-05 concept stage. The client's proven hooks plus category-level patterns from the org store become priors. Empirical priors replace cold starts.
→ W-02 · W-05

B1
Deliverable inventory. Query Notion for the period's completed items tagged to the brief. Clients forget what you shipped — showing it moves NPS.

04 — Fabric

The layer underneath.

What every workflow shares. Build this once. Every future agent inherits it for free.

Top-1% agencies don't diverge on the agents. They diverge on the layer underneath — the primitives that let the agents compound.
01 · contract
The contract
One typed Pydantic object per engagement — the brief, the envelope, the lineage. Append-only with provenance on every field. Notion is the human view. Every workflow reads from it, writes back to it. No agent paraphrases another.
02 · memory & evals
What the agency knows
Per-client voice golden sets (20 hand-curated examples, built in onboarding) plus org-level pattern memory (winning hooks across every client, anonymised). New clients inherit day-one priors. The compounding asset.
03 · skills
Versioned capability
Every skill has a schema, a test, a permission scope, a semver. No skill without a test. Grounded in the anthropics/skills format.
04 · hitl
Approval gates
Caps, not walls. Batched reviews. Rich Slack packets. Interrupt-and-resume via Inngest. The compliance gate (W-04) is the only hard wall.
05 · observability
Traces, cost, improvement
Langfuse traces every LLM call. Cost rolled up per deliverable so we know which workflows are actually profitable. Weekly cron clusters human edits to agent output and updates prompts — the agent that improves other agents.

One orchestrator, not two

Inngest for everything agentic — durable execution, typed events, retries, checkpoints. n8n kept strictly at the edges for simple client-facing automations (form intake, Zap-replacements). Two orchestrators is two failure modes and two debug surfaces. Pick one for the brain.

Langfuse alone

Traces, prompts, evals, cost rollup, online evaluation. Helicone dropped. Langfuse is the better choice for agentic workflows in 2026.

07 — Appendix

Deferred.

Two workflows that belong in the system eventually, but aren't in the core seven. Built only when the volume justifies the complexity.

A · Proposal generation

Template-based with LLM-filled slots. pgvector retrieval over won proposals filtered by outcome=won AND industry~client.industry. Parallel slot filling. Deterministic pricing stays out of the LLM — rules evaluated from brief.deliverables[] in code. Reviewer pass in fresh context. Same-day turnaround when built.

Build when: deal volume exceeds ~3 proposals/week or Adrian is the bottleneck.

B · Competitor & trend intelligence

Weekly cron per client. Apify scrapes Meta Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center, competitor landing pages. Embedding diff vs last week identifies what's new. Digest written to brief context and posted as a weekly "three competitor hooks worth stealing" Slack message to the client channel.

Build when: W-07 is mature and the core seven are stable.
