Agentic system blueprint

An operating system for Corient.

Five agent domains. Eleven workflows. One typed contract underneath them — the brief — that every agent reads from.

01 — Domains

Five agents. One contract.

The five domains are already deployed. The brief is not a sixth domain — it is the typed schema every agent reads from. Promoted to the fabric.

Not an agent. A typed schema. Lives in Notion as the human view, in Postgres as the source of truth. Every agent reads from it, writes back to it.
02 — Workflows

Eleven builds.

Each workflow is a standalone architecture. Each ships value on its own. Built in priority order, they compound into the full system.

W-01

The brief contract.

A typed schema, not a document. Lives in the client card in Notion. Auto-updates on every signal. Classified by change type, not a confidence score.

Every client in Corient's Notion has a card. The brief is a typed Pydantic schema projected into that card. Meetings, emails and Slack decisions all update the same object. Notion is the human view. The typed object is the source of truth that every other workflow reads programmatically.

Pipeline
1
Signal fires. Meeting ends · email arrives · Slack decision · form submitted · manual Notion edit.
Fathom · Gmail

Claude · Inngest
3
Meeting log appended. Transcript summarised, decisions extracted, action items tagged. Dated entry with a link to the full transcript.
Claude · Notion
4
Typed change classifier. Every diff is classified — factual (auto-merge + audit), interpretive (auto-merge + Slack notify), structural (HITL), contradictory (HITL, surface both versions). No confidence thresholds.
Claude Opus
✓
Review gate — structural or contradictory only. Slack message: "Brief update for Acme Co — structural change to deliverables. Approve, edit, or ignore." Factual and interpretive updates land silently with an audit entry.

Postgres · Notion

W-04

Social content
repurposing.

One long-form source becomes 30+ platform-native pieces. Voice fidelity enforced by per-client golden sets, not generic rubrics.

Extraction is solved. The hard problem is voice fidelity at platform shift — a LinkedIn paragraph and a TikTok hook for the same client must sound like the same brain. The only reliable answer is a per-client golden set of 20 hand-curated examples, scored by embedding distance plus rubric grading. Judges drift. Golden sets do not.

W-05

Compliance
& claims gate.

A hard wall, not a cap. Every generative asset passes through before delivery. This is how agencies avoid getting sued.

Generative ads at scale without a compliance pass is a lawsuit waiting to happen. Beauty claims, financial claims, supplement claims, health claims, restricted categories, Meta's prohibited content list, ASIC/ACCC for AU clients, FTC endorsement rules, age-gating, region-specific supers. Built once at org level so every client inherits the same policy brain.

Pipeline
1
Asset arrives. Final deliverable from W-03, W-04 or W-07. Before any Gate 2.
Inngest event
2
Claims extraction. Every explicit and implicit claim pulled from copy, voiceover, and on-screen text. Listed structurally.
Claude Sonnet
3
Substantiation cross-check. Each claim cross-referenced against the client's substantiation library (Mem0). Unsupported claims flagged.
Mem0 · Claude
4
Policy classifier — platform + region. Meta, TikTok, Google, LinkedIn prohibited content lists. ACCC/ASIC for AU, FTC for US, ASA for UK. Category-specific rules (alcohol, gambling, finance, health).
Rule engine
5
Auto-remediation or block. Missing legal super → auto-added via Creatomate template layer. Unsupported claim → hard block, human review.
Creatomate
✓
Any flagged item → human compliance review. Hard wall. Cannot be overridden by caps or budgets. Review goes to the account lead, not a random approver.
Slack · Notion
6
Audit log entry. Every asset carries a compliance receipt — claims list, substantiation sources, policy matches, approver. Legally defensible.

W-06

Video ad production.

Two pipelines, chosen by brief. Native generative for 80% of output. Segment-based for talking-head UGC only.

Veo 3.1 and Runway Gen-4.5 now handle 8–12 second coherent shots with consistent characters and native audio. For most ad formats this means generating end-to-end shots and assembling two or three — not stitching avatar + b-roll + VO segments. The segment-based pattern is reserved for talking-head and UGC-style formats where the client's CAC depends on a recognisable spokesperson.

W-09

Client reports.

Evidence cards, not free prose. Every claim cites a specific metric drop, a creative refresh date, or a tracked platform incident. No evidence, no claim.

Free narrative synthesis from metrics is the highest hallucination risk in the entire system. An agent saying "TikTok performance dropped because of creative fatigue" when the real cause was a Meta tracking outage is the kind of thing that ends a retainer. The fix is structural: the LLM fills slots in a constrained template, and every slot must cite a specific evidence card.

Pipeline
1
Unified metrics pull. GA4, Meta, TikTok, Google Ads, LinkedIn → canonical MetricPoint → Postgres.
n8n · Postgres
2
Anomaly detection. Statistical — z-score, 3-week moving average. Produces evidence cards: timestamp, metric, magnitude, tracked context (creative refresh, platform incident, budget change).
Code
3
Deliverable inventory. Query Notion for the period's completed items tagged to the brief.
Notion
4
Constrained slot-filling. Claude fills templated slots: {anomaly} {magnitude} {likely_cause_from_evidence} {recommended_action}. Every cause must cite an evidence card. No evidence → no claim. Hallucination becomes structurally impossible.
Claude Sonnet
✓
Human review. Reports are trust artefacts. Human always sees the final narrative.
Notion
5
Delivery. Branded PDF. Email TL;DR. Live Notion embed.
React-PDF

W-10

Proposal generation.

Template-based with LLM-filled slots. Deterministic pricing stays out of the LLM. Same-day turnaround, zero AI slop.

Free RAG-grounded writing over past proposals hallucinates boilerplate from competing clients. The fix is templates with slotted generation — section by section, each slot filled from the brief plus retrieved exemplars. Pricing stays deterministic because the one place stochastic reasoning has zero upside and lawsuit-grade downside is cost.

Pipeline

4
Parallel slot filling. One Sonnet call per slot. Each fed the template block + 3 exemplar answers from won proposals + brief context. Few-shot beats free generation.
Claude Sonnet
5

04 — Fabric

The layer underneath.

What every workflow shares. Build this once. Every future agent inherits it for free.

Top-1% agencies don't diverge on the agents. They diverge on the layer underneath — the primitives that let the agents compound.
brief contract
Typed schema
Pydantic schema in Postgres, Notion projection for humans. Every workflow reads from it programmatically. Not freeform text.
job envelope
Shared state
Append-only object per engagement. No agent paraphrases another. Hold the line on this during implementation.
memory
Per-client + org
Mem0 per-client. Postgres + pgvector for org-level pattern memory — winning hooks across every client, anonymised. New clients inherit day-one priors.
voice golden sets
Per-client eval
20 hand-curated examples per client. Built in onboarding. Scored by embedding distance + rubric. The single biggest content quality lever.
skills registry
Versioned, tested
Every skill has a schema, a test, a permission scope, a semver. No skill without a test.
hitl
Caps, not walls
Batched reviews. Rich Slack packets. Interrupt-and-resume. Compliance gate (W-05) is the only hard wall.
cost telemetry
Per-deliverable
Cost per winning static, per qualified report, per signed proposal. Priced from Langfuse traces. Tells us which workflows are actually profitable.
meta-improvement loop
Weekly
Cron pulls all human edits to agent output. Clusters them. If three clients in one week edited "headline too long" on W-03, the headline prompt needs an update. The agent that improves other agents.

One orchestrator, not two

Inngest for everything agentic — durable execution, typed events, retries, checkpoints. n8n kept strictly at the edges for simple client-facing automations (form intake, Zap-replacements). Two orchestrators is two failure modes and two debug surfaces. Pick one for the brain.

Langfuse alone

Traces, prompts, evals, cost rollup, online evaluation. Helicone dropped. Langfuse is the better choice for agentic workflows in 2026.
